Module 2 · Diagnose

Gravity Detector

Not everything that feels like a problem is solvable. If it isn't actionable, it isn't a problem — it's a condition.

Test Your Problem

Common Gravity Problems

These are real — but they're conditions, not problems. Stop fighting them. Reframe around them.

›
"I started too late."
⟶ Multiple futures still exist. You now have something most 25-year-olds don't: lived context. The transition is the credential.
›
"People my age don't switch careers."
⟶ That's a statistic, not a rule. The only career that matters is yours. One proof point invalidates the whole claim.
›
"My industry doesn't respect writers/strategists."
⟶ You're leaving that industry. The new one does — you just haven't shown them what you can do yet.

Module 5 · Reframe

Dysfunctional Belief Reframer

You can't design a new life while running the old belief system. Reframe the thought — then act on the reframe.

Reframe Your Own Belief
Alternative Interpretation

One action this unlocks
Built-in Reframes
›
"My degree determines my future."
⟶ Most people don't work in their major. Your career is not a destination — it's a design problem. You have more agency than this belief allows.
›
"If I'm successful, I'll be happy."
⟶ Causality runs the other way just as often. Design your happiness intentionally rather than earning it as a reward for achievement.
›
"It's too late to change."
⟶ Multiple futures still exist. The question isn't "is there time?" — it's "which future do I want to start building today?"
›
"We judge life by outcomes."
⟶ Life is process. Outcomes are confirmation bias — you remember the outcomes that validate your current story. The process is what you actually live.
›
"I need clarity before I can act."
⟶ Clarity comes after action, not before it. You build your way into knowing — you don't think your way into it. The prototype is the answer.
›
"Midlife people don't reinvent themselves."
⟶ Midlife is precisely when reinvention becomes possible — because the cost of ignoring the call finally outweighs the cost of changing. Your audience knows this intimately.

Module 7 · Create

Prototype Lab

Don't decide. Test. A prototype is the smallest possible version of an action that generates real-world feedback.

💬
Conversation Prototype

Talk to someone who lives the life you're designing. No pitching. Only learning.

🧪
Experience Prototype

Try the actual thing — even a tiny version. Insight from doing beats insight from imagining.

👁️
Shadow Prototype

Observe someone doing the work. Sit in on their day. Map what the reality actually looks like.

🛠️
Side Project Prototype

Build something tiny. A post, a tool, a free offer. Ship before you're ready.

The Letting Go Protocol

Once you lock a decision: write it down, date it, and stop revisiting it for 30 days. The obsessing after choosing is where energy goes to die. Commitment isn't certainty — it's forward motion despite uncertainty.
